What is genetic engineering?

Before you understand what genetic engineering is...

You must understand what genes are. Genes are long strands, or segments, of DNA (Deoxyribonecleic Acid) that identify certain traits in an organism, for instance blue eyes or brown hair.

What is DNA?

You may be thinking, "Well I know what genes are, to some degree, but what's this DNA that you were talking about?" Allow me to explain. DNA is a collection of nitrogen bases, which in turn are collections of molecules of Thymine, Guanine, Cytosine, Adenine, and Uracil. What I just named, the molecules, are elements, and you probably know what an element is.

Onto the real question...

Now, you may be thinking, "Well, this is all fine and dandy, but what is genetic engineering?!" Genetic engineering is the manipulation of aforementioned genes, whether by transplanting them cross-species, or by changing the nucleotide bases, to change what the gene is "read" as when it is copied by the messenger RNA, brought to the ribosome to be translated into proteins by the transfer RNA.

Random facts that don't fit anywhere else!

  1. Genetic manipulation happens naturally, to a certain degree.
  2. DNA is actually a collection of simple sugars and phosphate groupings.
  3. The gene was chemically explained, in 1953, by Francis Criek and Jamie Swatson.
  4. Genetic engineering became possible in 1954, shortly after the gene was "discovered".
